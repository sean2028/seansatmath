Exponential functions

Basic form

$y=a(b)^x$
  • $a$ = initial value
  • $b$ = growth/decay factor
  • $b>1$ means growth
  • $0
Example: $y=5(1.3)^x$ grows by 30% each time $x$ increases by 1.

Growth / decay models

Growth: $y=a(1+r)^t$
Decay: $y=a(1-r)^t$

$r$ is the decimal rate.

12% growth → 1.1225% decrease → 0.75

Doubling / halving

Doubling: $y=a\cdot2^{t/k}$
Halving: $y=a\left(\frac12\right)^{t/k}$

$k$ is the doubling or halving time, in the same units as $t$.

Exponent rules

RuleMeaning
$a^m\cdot a^n=a^{m+n}$Multiply same base → add exponents
$\dfrac{a^m}{a^n}=a^{m-n}$Divide same base → subtract
$(a^m)^n=a^{mn}$Power to a power → multiply
$a^0=1$Nonzero base
$a^{-n}=\dfrac1{a^n}$Negative exponent flips
$a^{1/2}=\sqrt a$Fractional exponent
$a^{m/n}=\sqrt[n]{a^m}$Numerator = power, denominator = root

Solving exponential equations

Same base:

$2^{x+3}=2^7\Rightarrow x+3=7\Rightarrow x=4$

Rewrite base:

$4^x=2^{10}$
$(2^2)^x=2^{10}$
$2x=10\Rightarrow x=5$

Logarithms

Logs = inverse of exponents

$\log_b(x)=y\Longleftrightarrow b^y=x$

A log asks: what power gives this result?

Conditions: $b>0$, $b\ne1$, $x>0$.

$\log_2(8)=3$ because $2^3=8$.

SAT-level log facts

FactWhy
$\log_b(1)=0$$b^0=1$
$\log_b(b)=1$$b^1=b$
$\log_b(b^x)=x$Inverse operation

Common log rules

$\log_b(MN)$$\log_bM+\log_bN$
$\log_b(M/N)$$\log_bM-\log_bN$
$\log_b(M^p)$$p\log_bM$
Change of base: $\log_b(x)=\dfrac{\log_k(x)}{\log_k(b)}$

Complex numbers

Imaginary unit

$i=\sqrt{-1}$
$i^2=-1$

General form:

$a+bi$

Powers of $i$

$i$→$-1$→$-i$→$1$

The cycle repeats every 4.

$i^{23}=i^3=-i$

Complex arithmetic

OperationRule
Add$(a+bi)+(c+di)=(a+c)+(b+d)i$
Subtract$(a+bi)-(c+di)=(a-c)+(b-d)i$
Multiply$(a+bi)(c+di)=(ac-bd)+(ad+bc)i$
Conjugate$(a+bi)(a-bi)=a^2+b^2$

Dividing by a complex number

Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate of the denominator.

$\dfrac{a+bi}{c+di}\cdot\dfrac{c-di}{c-di}$
Denominator becomes $c^2+d^2$

Quadratics with complex solutions

If the discriminant is negative, the solutions are complex.

$b^2-4ac<0$
$x^2+9=0\Rightarrow x^2=-9\Rightarrow x=\pm3i$

Mini practice bank

1. A value increases 8% each year from 500. Write the model.
$y=500(1.08)^t$.
2. Solve $9^x=3^{12}$.
$9=3^2$, so $3^{2x}=3^{12}$, $2x=12$, $x=6$.
3. Evaluate $\log_5(125)$.
$\log_5(125)=3$ because $5^3=125$.
